In order to make the artwork more accessible, some museums provide audio guides and tactile plates. Whilst these methods do improve the accessibility of the artwork, they fail to create an aesthetic experience for the individual. This work introduces an automatic system designed to improve the accessibility of visual art by creating this aesthetic experience for people with vision impairments. It does this by analysing a piece of art and creating a musical experience that conveys the perceived emotion as well as information about the scenery depicted in the artwork.
